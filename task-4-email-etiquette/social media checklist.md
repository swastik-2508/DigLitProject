# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 4 Part B**
**Swastik Kumar Barik | 25BOE10064**

---

## ✅ Do's

1. **Keep professional profiles updated** — LinkedIn and GitHub should reflect your current skills, projects, and education at all times.
2. **Think before you post** — Ask yourself whether you would be comfortable with a future employer, professor, or family member seeing the content.
3. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** — Add an extra layer of security to all accounts, especially email and banking apps.
4. **Give credit for others' work** — If you share someone's code, writing, or idea, always attribute the original creator.
5. **Use platforms purposefully** — Use GitHub and Kaggle to document your learning publicly; let your online presence tell a story of growth.

---

## ❌ Don'ts

1. **Don't share personal identifiers publicly** — Avoid posting your college ID number, phone number, home address, or daily schedule on any public platform.
2. **Don't spread unverified information** — Do not forward news, screenshots, or claims without verifying them from a credible source first.
3. **Don't reuse passwords** — Using the same password across multiple platforms means one breach exposes everything.
4. **Don't accept unknown connection requests blindly** — Verify the identity of anyone you connect with, especially on LinkedIn and GitHub.
5. **Don't post grievances publicly** — Complaints about professors, classmates, exams, or your institution should never appear on public social media. Use official channels instead.
