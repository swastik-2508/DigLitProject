## What is UPI/Online Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of financial cybercrime where attackers deceive victims into authorising fraudulent transactions or revealing sensitive credentials such as UPI PINs, OTPs, or linked bank account details. Unlike traditional bank fraud, UPI transactions are near-instant and largely irreversible, making this crime especially damaging.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Contact** — The attacker reaches the victim via phone call, WhatsApp, or SMS, posing as a bank representative, government official, or known acquaintance.
2. **Pretext** — They create urgency: "Your account will be blocked," "You have received a cashback — accept it now," or "I need to send you money, please scan this QR code."
3. **Deception** — The victim is sent a *collect request* (a request for money, not a payment) or a QR code. The attacker frames it as "receiving" money.
4. **Execution** — The victim scans the QR code or approves the collect request and enters their UPI PIN, unknowingly authorising a payment *out* of their account.
5. **Disappearance** — The money is transferred instantly. The attacker disconnects and the funds are moved through multiple accounts before the victim realises.

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

Students and first-time UPI users, elderly individuals unfamiliar with digital payments, and people actively selling items on platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace (where "buyers" offer to pay via QR code).

## Consequences

Victims lose money instantly with very low chances of recovery. Beyond financial loss, victims experience stress, embarrassment, and a loss of trust in digital payment systems. In some cases, repeated fraud through the same account can lead to the account being flagged or frozen by the bank.
