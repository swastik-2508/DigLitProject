## ✅ General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — a password manager like Bitwarden can help you manage them without memorising each one.
2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, and college portals.
3. **Never click links in unsolicited emails or SMS messages** — go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself.
4. **Keep your devices and apps updated** — security patches fix known vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit.
5. **Do not share OTPs with anyone** — no bank, government body, or legitimate company will ever ask for your OTP over a call or message.
6. **Check website URLs carefully** before entering login credentials — look for `https://` and verify the domain spelling (e.g., `sbi.co.in` not `sbi-login.net`).

---

## 💸 Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory)

7. **Scanning a QR code always means you are paying money, never receiving it** — if someone says "scan this to receive your payment," it is a scam.
8. **Never approve a UPI collect request from an unknown number** — a collect request is a demand for money from your account, not a transfer to you.

---

## 🚨 Reporting Cybercrime in India

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (available 24x7)

Report immediately if you suspect fraud — early reporting increases the chance of freezing the fraudulent transaction before the money is moved.
